### Introduction to the Annotations of the OpenClaw Manifesto
The essence of this manifesto is not a marketing or promotion of the tool OpenClaw, but a fundamental cognitive reconstruction of the "relationship between humans and AI tools."

Most of the praise and criticism surrounding this manifesto and this tool in the public sphere remain on an extremely superficial level: either deifying the tool or demonizing it, always discussing "whether the tool is easy to use or powerful," but never touching the real core—the ultimate value of an AI tool is never determined by the tool itself, but by the user's cognition, ability, and self-control.

Those who praise the tool as a salvation and those who belittle it as a flood share essentially the same mindset: they all attribute their success or failure, strength or weakness, and the initiative of their lives to external things rather than themselves. All the sense of power in this manifesto does not come from the tool OpenClaw, but from the user's clear self-awareness, systematic personal abilities, and absolute adherence to the core that "humans are always the end, and tools are always the means."
